Participant Information Sheet

You are being invited to take part in a research survey. Before you decide to take part, it is important for you to understand why the research is being done and what it will involve. Please read the following information carefully and discuss it with others if you wish. Take some time to decide whether or not you wish to take part.


What is the purpose of the study?

The Participatory Harm Auditing Workbenches and Methodologies (PHAWM) project aims to develop tools and processes that help people without a technical background to audit (that is, check and assess) AI systems to see how well they work and whether they are, among other things, fair, accurate, and reliable. In this survey, you will be asked questions about interfaces that we designed to help people audit search results. We are interested in how useful and beneficial you find the designs in helping you discover assess search results.


What will happen if I take part?

You will be entering an online survey (using Qualtrics) anonymously and click through the designed interfaces. You will be asked a few questions about usability of each interface after you have visited it. Only your survey responses will be captured. Your interactions and clicks on the interface will not be captured.


Do I have to take part?

Participation in the survey is voluntary. You can stop at any stage of the survey. However, once you have submitted your responses, you will no longer be able to withdraw your data.


Will my taking part in the study be kept confidential?

All survey responses will be anonymised.


What will happen to the results?

The anonymous results of the survey will be included in reports and publications. Other researchers associated with this project may access the anonymous results for analysis and reporting.


What are the possible disadvantages and risks of taking part?

There are no risks or side effects for taking part in this survey.


What are the possible benefits of taking part?

By taking part in the survey, you will contribute to help us build better AI auditing systems.


How is the project being funded?

The project is funded by Responsible AI UK.
